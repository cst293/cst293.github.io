Who?

Welcome to my homepage. I am Catherine Thorbecke, a humanitarian multimedia journalist.

I am half-Japanese, half-American. I was born right outside of Washington, D.C., grew up mostly in Tokyo, but attended high school in Hawaii. Recently spent a year in London and am currently based in New York City.

I currently work at ABC News National and as a copy-editor for Human Rights Now, a human rights advocacy NGO based in Tokyo with offices in New York and Geneva.

Previously of the Mainichi Newspapers, the oldest national daily newspaper in Japan and the only Japanese news organization to have won a Pulitzer Prize.

Some may still know me as the Sir Wilfred’s Barista from Maui’s greatest coffee shop.

Passionate about writing, story-telling, giving a voice to the voiceless and highlighting abuses of power or human rights violations. Also a foodie.

Feel free to reach out to me with tips, stories, business inquiries and love letters at catherine.s.thorbecke@abc.com

You can also follow me on Twitter.
